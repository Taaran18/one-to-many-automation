Introduction

This document addresses the most common questions that businesses, marketing teams, and decision-makers ask about OneToMany — our WhatsApp outreach and campaign automation platform. Each answer has been crafted to give you a clear, complete understanding of how the platform works and the value it delivers to your organisation.

Note: If you have a question not covered in this document, please reach out to your dedicated account representative. We are happy to provide a personalised walkthrough or live demonstration at your convenience.

General Questions

Q1. What is OneToMany?
OneToMany is a full-stack WhatsApp outreach and campaign automation platform built for businesses that need to communicate with a large number of contacts efficiently and personally. It allows you to manage leads, design personalised message templates, schedule bulk campaigns, and handle two-way conversations — all from a single, easy-to-use dashboard. Think of it as a WhatsApp-native CRM and marketing engine combined into one.
Q2. Who is OneToMany built for?
OneToMany is designed for small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs), marketing agencies, sales teams, freelancers, and any organisation that relies on WhatsApp to communicate with customers, leads, or prospects at scale. If your team is currently sending WhatsApp messages manually one-by-one, OneToMany eliminates that effort entirely.
Q3. Do I need any technical knowledge to use OneToMany?
No. The platform is designed with a clean, intuitive interface that any business user can navigate without technical training. Setting up a campaign involves three simple steps: add your contacts, choose a message template, and schedule when to send it. Your account team will handle initial setup and onboarding.
Q4. Is OneToMany a WhatsApp-approved solution?
OneToMany supports two modes of WhatsApp connectivity:
  • QR Code Mode — uses WhatsApp Web (the same technology your phone uses to connect to web.whatsapp.com). This mode is instant, free, and requires no Meta Business approval.
  • Meta Cloud API Mode — the official WhatsApp Business API provided by Meta, suitable for larger enterprises requiring template pre-approval and higher throughput. This mode is fully approved by Meta.
We recommend the appropriate mode based on your business volume and compliance needs.

Features & Functionality

Q5. Can I personalise messages for each contact?
Yes. Every message template in OneToMany supports dynamic personalisation using variable placeholders. For example, a template like "Hi {{name}}, your order from {{company}} is ready!" will automatically be filled with each contact's actual name and company when the campaign runs. You map each variable to the corresponding contact field in your database — no manual editing required.
Q6. Can I schedule campaigns in advance?
Absolutely. OneToMany includes a powerful scheduling engine that lets you:
  • Send a one-time campaign at a specific date and time.
  • Set up recurring campaigns that run daily, weekly (on chosen days), or monthly (on a chosen day of the month).
  • Define an optional end date so recurring campaigns stop automatically.
The platform's background scheduler runs continuously and fires campaigns at precisely the right time — even if you're not logged in.
Q7. Can I target different groups of contacts with different campaigns?
Yes. OneToMany lets you organise contacts into named Lead Groups (e.g., "VIP Customers", "Pending Renewals", "Trade Show Leads"). When creating a campaign, you select one or more groups as the target audience. The same contact can belong to multiple groups, and you can update group membership at any time.
Q8. Can leads reply to my messages?
Yes. OneToMany features a built-in two-way inbox that displays all conversations in a WhatsApp-like interface. When a contact replies to a campaign message, their response appears in your Chats inbox immediately. You can read and reply directly from the platform without switching to your phone or WhatsApp Web.
Q9. How many contacts can I manage on the platform?
The platform itself does not impose a hard cap on the number of contacts. You can import and manage thousands of leads, organise them across multiple groups, and run campaigns to all of them. Practical limits may apply depending on your WhatsApp connection mode and the server plan you are on. Your account team will advise on the right configuration for your volume.
Q10. Can I import my existing contacts into the platform?
Yes. OneToMany supports bulk contact import, allowing you to upload your existing customer or lead list at once. Individual contacts can also be added manually through the interface. Each contact record supports rich profile data including name, phone number, email address, company, physical address, tags, and status (e.g., prospect, customer).
Q11. What types of message templates does the platform support?
OneToMany supports two types of templates aligned with your WhatsApp connection mode:
  • Standard Templates (QR Mode): Free-text messages with named variable placeholders. You write the message body as you would normally, and variables are resolved per contact at send time.
  • Meta API Templates: Structured templates with separate Header (text or image), Body (with numbered parameters), Footer, and interactive Buttons (URL links, phone-call buttons, quick reply buttons). These are submitted to Meta for approval before use.
Q12. Can I resend or duplicate a campaign?
Yes. Any campaign can be duplicated with one click, creating a copy that you can adjust and reschedule without starting from scratch. Completed campaigns can also be re-run manually when needed.

WhatsApp Connection

Q13. How do I connect my WhatsApp account to OneToMany?
There are two options:
  1. QR Code Mode: Click "Connect WhatsApp" in the platform, scan the displayed QR code using your WhatsApp mobile app (just like WhatsApp Web), and you're connected within seconds. Your session is saved, so you won't need to scan again after a restart.
  2. Meta Cloud API Mode: Enter your Meta Business Phone ID, Access Token, and WhatsApp Business Account ID — credentials available from your Meta Business Manager. The platform validates these instantly and stores them securely.
Q14. What happens if my WhatsApp connection drops?
In QR Code mode, the platform automatically attempts to restore your saved session on restart, so brief interruptions do not require re-scanning. If a full disconnection occurs, you will see a status indicator in the navigation bar and can re-connect with one click. Any campaigns scheduled during the downtime will resume once connectivity is restored.
Q15. Is there a difference in cost between the two connection modes?
QR Code mode has no per-message fees beyond your standard WhatsApp usage. Meta Cloud API mode follows Meta's conversation-based pricing model, where charges apply per 24-hour conversation window. For high-volume enterprise clients, Meta's official pricing page provides current rates. Your account team can help you determine the most cost-effective approach for your expected message volume.

Security & Privacy

Q16. How is my data protected?
OneToMany is built with multiple layers of security:
  • Authentication: All access is protected by JSON Web Token (JWT) authentication. Sessions expire automatically.
  • Encrypted Credentials: Meta API access tokens are encrypted at rest using AES-128 (Fernet) encryption before storage in the database. They are never stored as plain text.
  • Data Isolation: Every user's data (leads, templates, campaigns, messages) is completely isolated from other users in the system.
  • Secure Transport: All communication between the application and external APIs (including WhatsApp) is conducted over HTTPS.
  • Database Security: Data is stored in Supabase (PostgreSQL), a managed cloud database with automatic SSL encryption in transit.
Q17. Who can see my contacts and campaign data?
Only users authenticated to your account can access your data. OneToMany enforces strict per-user data isolation at the database level — your contacts, templates, campaigns, and message history are never visible to other organisations or users on the platform.
Q18. Does OneToMany store the messages sent to my contacts?
Yes. The platform maintains a message log for every outbound campaign message and every inbound reply. This gives you a complete audit trail and conversation history. These logs are stored securely in your account's isolated database partition and can be viewed at any time in the Chats inbox.

Analytics & Reporting

Q19. What analytics does OneToMany provide?
The platform's Dashboard provides real-time insights including:
  • Total leads, templates, and campaigns in your account.
  • Number of leads who have received at least one campaign message (Leads Touched).
  • Total messages sent in the current month.
  • Customer vs. prospect breakdown across your lead database.
  • Campaigns scheduled for the next 7 days and next 24 hours.
  • A 30-day rolling chart of daily message volume.
Q20. Can I track whether my messages were delivered?
Yes. Every message sent through OneToMany is logged with a delivery status (Sent, Delivered, or Failed). You can review the status of each recipient in any campaign's message log. This lets you identify failed deliveries and take corrective action (such as updating the contact's phone number).

Onboarding & Support

Q21. How quickly can we go live?
For QR Code mode, the platform can be live and sending its first campaign within the same day as onboarding — there is no approval waiting period. For Meta Cloud API mode, go-live depends on Meta's template approval timeline, which typically takes 24–72 hours. Your account team will coordinate with you on setup and guide you through each step.
Q22. What kind of support is available?
We provide dedicated onboarding support including platform configuration, contact import assistance, template creation guidance, and a walkthrough of all features. Ongoing support is available through your account representative. Enterprise clients have access to priority technical support.
Q23. Can the platform scale as our business grows?
Yes. OneToMany is built on a scalable cloud infrastructure. As your contact database grows or your campaign frequency increases, the underlying system scales to meet demand. We will work with you proactively to ensure your plan and infrastructure match your evolving needs.

1. Executive Summary

OneToMany is a comprehensive, cloud-hosted WhatsApp outreach and campaign automation platform engineered to help businesses scale their customer communication without increasing headcount. The platform eliminates the manual, error-prone process of sending individual WhatsApp messages by providing a centralised hub for lead management, template authoring, campaign scheduling, and two-way conversation management.

Designed with both operational simplicity and enterprise-grade security in mind, OneToMany is built on a modern technology stack and supports two methods of WhatsApp connectivity — the QR Code mode for immediate deployment and the official Meta Cloud API for regulated or high-volume environments.

Intended Audience: This proposal is intended for business owners, operations leads, marketing directors, and IT decision-makers evaluating the platform for deployment within their organisation.

2. Platform Overview

OneToMany is a multi-module, web-based platform accessible from any modern browser. It consists of three interconnected services:

Component Role Technology
Web Application (Frontend) User interface — dashboards, forms, inbox, analytics Next.js 16, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS
API Server (Backend) Business logic, data management, scheduling, security FastAPI (Python), PostgreSQL via Supabase
WhatsApp Bridge WhatsApp connectivity, message sending, incoming message handling Node.js, Express, whatsapp-web.js

All three components are deployed on cloud infrastructure (Render / Vercel) and communicate over secured HTTPS channels. The database layer uses Supabase-managed PostgreSQL with automatic SSL encryption in transit.

3. Feature Modules

The following sections describe each functional module of the OneToMany platform in detail.

3.1 Lead Management

Description

The Lead Management module serves as the contact database of the platform. Businesses can store, organise, and maintain detailed records of all their contacts — customers, prospects, leads, or partners.

Capabilities

  • Add, edit, and delete individual contact records.
  • Rich contact profiles: name, phone number, email address, company, physical address (street, city, state, country, pincode), tags, and status.
  • Contact statuses: Prospect, Customer, and custom classifications.
  • Tag system for flexible, keyword-based organisation of contacts.
  • Full-text search across name, phone, and email fields.
  • Bulk contact import via structured data upload.
  • Lead Groups: create named groups (e.g., "Q1 Campaign Targets") and assign leads to one or more groups.
  • Dynamic group membership — add or remove leads at any time.

3.2 Message Template Builder

Description

The Template Builder allows marketing teams to design reusable message templates that can be personalised dynamically for each contact. The platform supports two template types aligned with the connected WhatsApp mode.

Standard Templates (QR Mode)

  • Free-text message body with support for named variable placeholders (e.g., {{name}}, {{company}}).
  • Variables are automatically resolved from each contact's profile at send time.
  • Tag and organise templates by category.
  • Preview resolved messages with sample contact data before sending.

Meta API Templates

  • Structured layout with independent components: Header (text or image), Body (with numbered parameters), Footer, and Buttons.
  • Button types supported: URL link buttons, phone-call buttons, and quick-reply buttons.
  • Variable mapping: map numbered parameters (e.g., {{1}}) to specific contact profile fields.
  • Image header upload via drag-and-drop interface (JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF).
  • Direct submission to Meta for approval from within the platform.
  • Real-time approval status tracking (Pending, Approved, Rejected).

3.3 Campaign Engine & Scheduler

Description

The Campaign Engine is the core operational module of OneToMany. It enables businesses to configure, schedule, and automate the delivery of messages to selected contact groups at precisely defined times — with support for both one-time and recurring campaigns.

Campaign Configuration

  • Select one message template and one or more target Lead Groups per campaign.
  • Set a specific send date and time.
  • Optional campaign description and internal tags for organisation.

Scheduling Options

Schedule Type Description
One-Time Campaign fires once at the scheduled date and time.
Daily Campaign repeats every day at the set time.
Weekly Campaign repeats on selected day(s) of the week.
Monthly Campaign repeats on a chosen day of each month.

Campaign Management

  • Optional stop date to automatically end recurring campaigns.
  • Campaign status lifecycle: Draft → Scheduled → Running → Completed / Failed.
  • One-click campaign duplication to speed up creation of similar campaigns.
  • Manual re-run capability for any completed campaign.
  • Per-recipient message logs with delivery status (Sent, Delivered, Failed).
  • Background scheduler checks and fires campaigns every 60 seconds — no manual intervention required.

3.4 WhatsApp Connection Manager

Description

The Connection Manager allows users to link their WhatsApp account to the platform using one of two modes, each suited to different business requirements.

QR Code Mode

  • Connect by scanning a QR code — exactly like WhatsApp Web.
  • No Meta Business account or API approval required.
  • Session is automatically saved and restored after platform restarts.
  • Real-time connection status displayed in the navigation bar.
  • Incoming message webhook — replies from contacts arrive in the inbox instantly.

Meta Cloud API Mode

  • Official WhatsApp Business API — suitable for higher volumes and regulated industries.
  • Requires a Meta Business Phone ID, Access Token, and WhatsApp Business Account ID.
  • Credentials validated in real-time against Meta's Graph API upon connection.
  • Access tokens encrypted at rest using AES-128 (Fernet) — never stored as plain text.
  • Supports structured Meta template messages with headers, footers, and buttons.

3.5 Two-Way Chat Inbox

Description

The Chat Inbox provides a familiar, WhatsApp-style interface for managing all inbound and outbound conversations in one place. Teams no longer need to switch between the platform and a phone to handle replies.

Capabilities

  • Unified conversation threads combining outbound campaign messages and inbound contact replies.
  • Contact list sorted by most recent message activity.
  • Unread message counters per conversation.
  • Mark conversations as read.
  • Full-text search within message threads.
  • Send freeform replies to any contact directly from the platform.
  • Real-time updates as new messages are received from contacts.

3.6 Dashboard & Analytics

Description

The Dashboard provides a real-time operational overview of the entire platform, giving decision-makers and marketing managers instant visibility into key performance metrics.

Metrics & Insights

  • Total leads, templates, and active campaigns in the account.
  • Leads Touched: number of unique contacts who have received at least one campaign message.
  • Total messages dispatched in the current calendar month.
  • Lead segmentation breakdown: Customer vs. Prospect counts.
  • Campaigns scheduled for the next 7 days — upcoming schedule at a glance.
  • Campaigns firing in the next 24 hours — same-day schedule view.
  • 30-Day Message Volume Chart: a bar/line chart of daily message output over the past 30 days for trend analysis.

3.7 Authentication & User Management

Description

OneToMany uses industry-standard security practices for user authentication and session management.

Capabilities

  • User registration and login via email address or phone number.
  • Passwords stored as secure hashes — never in plain text.
  • JSON Web Token (JWT) based session management with automatic expiry.
  • Protected routes — unauthenticated users are redirected to login automatically.
  • Auto-logout on session expiry or invalid token detection.
  • Full per-user data isolation: each account's data is logically separated at the database level.

3.8 User Interface & Experience

Capabilities

  • Dark / Light Mode: users can toggle between dark and light themes according to preference.
  • Responsive Design: the interface adapts for both desktop and mobile browser use.
  • Image Upload: drag-and-drop image upload for template headers (supports JPG, PNG, WebP, GIF formats).
  • Real-Time Status Indicators: WhatsApp connection status is displayed persistently in the navigation bar.
  • Tag Management: inline tag creation and management across leads, templates, and campaigns.
  • Form Validation: client-side and server-side validation on all input forms with clear error messaging.
  • API Documentation: interactive API documentation is auto-generated and available to technical users for integration purposes.

4. Technical Architecture

The platform follows a decoupled three-tier architecture. The frontend communicates with the backend exclusively via a secured REST API. The backend orchestrates all business logic, data persistence, and external integrations. The WhatsApp Bridge operates as an independent service, enabling clean separation of concerns.

Layer Technology Responsibility
Frontend Next.js 16 (React 19, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS v4) UI rendering, routing, JWT auth handling, API consumption
Backend API FastAPI (Python), SQLAlchemy ORM Business logic, authentication, scheduling, data management
Database PostgreSQL via Supabase (cloud-managed) Persistent data storage with SSL encryption in transit
WhatsApp Bridge Node.js, Express, whatsapp-web.js, Puppeteer QR code generation, session management, message relay
Scheduler APScheduler (embedded in backend) Campaign scheduling, background job execution (60-second tick)
File Handling Backend static file server Image upload and retrieval for template headers

8. Data Model Overview

The platform stores all data in a normalised relational database. The core entities and their relationships are summarised below:

Entity Description
User Registered account holder; owner of all data within the account
Lead Individual contact record with profile data, tags, and status
Lead Group Named collection of leads used for campaign targeting (many-to-many)
Template Reusable message template (Standard or Meta API format)
Campaign Scheduled outreach with template, target groups, recurrence settings, and status
Message Log Record of each message sent per recipient, per campaign run, with delivery status
Incoming Message Inbound reply or message received from a contact via WhatsApp
WhatsApp Session Connection record storing mode (QR / Meta), status, and encrypted credentials

9. Client Requirements & Prerequisites

To deploy and operate OneToMany, the following are required from the client side:

9.1 Minimum Requirements

  • A WhatsApp account (personal or business) with an active phone number — for QR Code mode.
  • A modern web browser (Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox, or Safari — latest versions).
  • Stable internet connection.
  • A list of contacts to import (name and phone number at minimum).

9.2 For Meta Cloud API Mode (Optional)

  • An active Meta Business Manager account.
  • A verified WhatsApp Business Account (WABA) with an associated phone number.
  • A permanent or long-lived Meta System User Access Token with appropriate permissions.
  • Pre-approved message templates submitted through Meta's template manager (or submitted via OneToMany).

9.3 Onboarding Information Required

  • Business name and primary contact details.
  • Initial contact list (CSV, Excel, or JSON format acceptable).
  • Initial message templates or content guidelines.
  • Campaign schedule and frequency preferences.

Continuous Updates: The platform is under active development. New features, performance improvements, and security patches are deployed regularly. Clients benefit from all updates automatically.
